Agricultural Field Officers.

About the Company
Rural AgriHub is a social enterprise integrating sellers from rural areas with buyers of fresh fruits, vegetables and general agricultural products in urban areas. We deliver a wide range of both organic and non-organic products directly from farms to stores of B2B customer retailers, wholesalers, food processors, restaurants, exporters and institutional kitchens. We support local producers, local communities and smallholder farmers on crop planning in alignment with marketplaces, upgrading farm side infrastructure and transition to sustainable practices as well as incorporating sustainability standards through farmer trainings.

About the Project
Regenerative Agriculture project in Tamale is a WhatIF Foods – Rural AgriHub Company Ltd and Pond Foundation partnership project. We seek to build a regenerative Bambara Groundnut (BGN) supply chain anchored in smallholder farmers’ fields in the Northern Region around Tamale, in 5 districts (Tolon, Kumbungu, Yendi, Bimbila and Karaga). The project will support smallholder farmers to increase their yields while improving their soils and community environment. The ultimate goal is to enhance farm family livelihoods and overall well-being, which will come from improved farming practices, increased yields and prices, sensitive cultural engagement, improved services and an improved community environment. As such we are looking for Agricultural Field Officers in each of the district to support and coordinate field activities with the smallholder farmer groups.

General responsibilities
Under the supervision of the Project Manager, the Agricultural Field Officer (AFO) is responsible for the daily technical planning, supervision, monitoring and reporting of the field activities of the Regenerative Bambara Value Chain Project and ensuring proper coordination between farmers, Community Facilitators (CFs), local authorities, district authorities and municipalities. The position requires extensive field work and constant movement in Kumbungu, Tolon, Yendi, Bimbila and Karaga districts.

Job descriptions for Agricultural Field Officer
• Identification and registration of farmers in project operational areas.
• Provide technical training to farmers on good agricultural practices (GAPs).
• Technical planning, supervision and monitoring of field activities.
• Ensure the timely implementation of field works as per the predefined schedule.
• Monitor the conformity of the field activities (trainings, field visits and meetings) with the standards of the
Project Production Manual, and as per the agreed planned activities.
• Support the Project Manager on the technical evaluation of agricultural training materials and subsequent implementation.
• Provide customized technical support to farmers who require a more hands-on approach, and assist with day-to-day problem-solving.
• Coordinate with local authorities and ensure continuous communication with farmers and Community Facilitators (CFs) regarding the implementation of the project in order to ensure cohesion and successful implementation of the project.
• Support farmers throughout the project life cycle, helping them to apply the knowledge, techniques and innovations promoted during trainings and conduct regular data collection and reporting for project progress monitoring.
• Perform other duties as may be assigned

Qualification and requirements
• Minimum of Certificate/Diploma/Bachelor’s degree in Agriculture/Agronomy or related fields.
• Minimum of 2 years’ experience working with smallholder farmers.
• Deep understanding of the formation of farmer groups/ associations.
• Considerable knowledge on crop management.
• Working knowledge on using Microsoft Office (word, excel, power point, etc.).

Excellent communication skills and willingness to learn.
• Willingness to work in rural areas.
• Ability to understand and speak the local language of the farmers in the project operational areas.
